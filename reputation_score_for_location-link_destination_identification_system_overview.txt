In the system, a reputation score would likely be calculated from a combination of **identity confidence**, **historical consistency**, **operational reliability**, and **independent verification**.

One possible formula might look like:

```text
Reputation Score =
  30% Identity Stability
+ 25% Arrival Accuracy
+ 20% Audit Results
+ 15% Network Consensus
+ 10% Historical Continuity
```

## Identity Stability

Measures how often the destination's identity has changed.

```text
Years without marker change
Number of reassignments
Unauthorized modifications
```

Example:

```text
Earth Port 01

8 years unchanged
0 unauthorized changes

Score: 99
```

versus

```text
Earth Port 01

5 reassignments this month

Score: 40
```

The more stable the identity, the higher the reputation.

***

## Arrival Accuracy

Tracks whether travelers actually arrive where they intended to go.

```text
Departures: 1,000,000
Successful Arrivals: 999,998

Accuracy:
99.9998%
```

Destinations with frequent routing discrepancies lose reputation.

***

## Audit Results

Independent inspectors periodically verify destination integrity.

```text
Audit Success Rate:
98 / 100
```

Recent failures might reduce reputation much more heavily than old failures.

For example:

```text
Passed yesterday: +5

Failed yesterday: -25
```

***

## Network Consensus

Measures agreement among independent authorities.

```text
Transport Authority:
Earth Port

Security Authority:
Earth Port

Regional Authority:
Earth Port
```

High agreement increases confidence.

Disagreement reduces score:

```text
Authority A:
Earth

Authority B:
Mars

Authority C:
Unknown
```

This suggests potential tampering.

***

## Historical Continuity

This is where reputation becomes especially useful for detecting marker switching.

Suppose:

```text
Marker A
served Earth
for 40 years
```

Suddenly:

```text
Earth
now points to Marker B
```

Even if technically authorized, the score decreases because the change deviates from historical behavior.

The system asks:

> Is this destination behaving like the destination we've known for decades?

***

## Time Weighting

Recent behavior matters more than ancient behavior.

For example:

```text
10-year-old failure:
-1 point

Failure today:
-20 points
```

This prevents very old incidents from permanently damaging a destination's reputation.

***

## Confidence Levels

The final score could be translated into trust categories.

```text
95-100  Trusted
85-94   Verified
70-84   Caution
50-69   Suspicious
0-49    Restricted
```

A transport system might refuse automatic routing to destinations below a certain threshold.

***

## Advanced Reputation Graph

A sophisticated implementation might score not only the destination but the entire ecosystem around it:

```text
Earth Port
├── Marker Reputation
├── Authority Reputation
├── Beacon Reputation
├── Link Reputation
└── Region Reputation
```

The final score becomes a weighted combination of all connected entities.

```text
Destination Reputation
      ↓
Trust Graph Evaluation
      ↓
Risk Assessment
      ↓
Transport Approval
```

In a marker-switching scenario, the most important role of reputation is that it provides **memory**. Authentication can verify that a marker is valid *right now*, but reputation can reveal that a destination's identity suddenly changed after decades of consistency. That makes reputation particularly effective at detecting any sort of "three-card monte" destination reassignment model.
